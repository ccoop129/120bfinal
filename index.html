同舟共济

Rethinking U.S.–China Business Dialogue

重新思考中美工商界对话

Scroll
一
陷阱 The Trap
History tells us: when a rising power meets an established one, the result is often conflict.
Ancient Athens and Sparta. One ascending. One defending.
Today, many are asking — could the United States and China find themselves in a similar place?
Graham Allison calls this the "Thucydides Trap" When a rising power challenges an established one, the structural tension can become catastrophic.
历史让我们有理由担心。 History gives us reason for concern.
但是，历史并不一定决定未来。 But history does not have to determine the future.
二
矛盾 The Contradiction
Today, the U.S.–China relationship is largely defined by competition. Trade restrictions. Technology rivalry. Strategic mistrust.
And yet — beneath the surface — another reality persists.
Supply chains still cross both countries. Capital still flows between them. Innovation still depends on shared talent, knowledge, and infrastructure.
· · ·
你中有我，我中有你。 You are in me, and I am in you.
吴越同舟。 Two rivals, in the same boat.
But if both countries are so deeply linked — why does it feel like they are drifting apart? Is decoupling already happening?
三
体系的压力 A System Under Strain
Traditionally, we assume governments manage relations between nations. But governments are focused on security, strategy, political pressures.
Businesses are different. They think about risk, opportunity, and long-term growth. They still want to develop, collaborate, and innovate.
Interdependence Geopolitical risk
Where businesses now operate — caught between economic logic and political pressure
The problem: there is no stable, trusted platform where businesses, investors, and researchers from both sides can openly discuss these tensions.
我们原本依赖的体系，正在承受越来越大的压力。 The systems we once relied on are under growing strain.
四
质疑 Pressure Test
Any honest proposal must face hard questions.
82%
of U.S. companies operating in China were still profitable in 2024.
USCBC Survey, 2025
<50%
remained optimistic about the future of their business in China.
USCBC Survey, 2025
Profits remain. Confidence has faltered. And confidence tends to predict the future before profits do.
A 2025 study found that declining bilateral trade figures may not capture the full picture — much trade now routes through Mexico, Vietnam, South Korea, and other third countries.
Decoupling has not disappeared. It has only been obscured.
In technology — chips, AI, advanced manufacturing — decoupling is already underway, and likely irreversible. Here, competition is closer to zero-sum.
这些都不是理论问题。而是真实存在的限制。 These are not theoretical problems. They are real constraints.
五
提案 The Proposal
If governments have their limits, and markets alone are not enough — what is missing?
We propose a dialogue platform for businesses, investors, and academic institutions. Not ideological. Focused on practical cooperation and coordination. Not to replace government. To complement it.
一
Find common ground
Identify areas still open to collaboration — green energy, medical technology, supply chain stability — while clarifying red lines so companies can assess risk clearly.
二
Bridge the information gap
Help businesses understand each other's institutions, policies, and commercial cultures. Many cross-border failures stem not from malice but from misunderstanding.
三
Shift public narratives
Through short video and storytelling, show the similarities and differences between U.S. and Chinese enterprises. Reduce stereotypes. Build more complete understanding.
四
Build lasting dialogue
Not a one-time conference, but a sustained process of exchange — so that relationships can form slowly, and trust can develop over time.
Phase 1 — Short-form media
Short videos introducing real business situations from both sides. Accessible, human, concrete.
Phase 2 — Small-group dialogue
Intimate conversations among entrepreneurs, investors, and researchers. Depth over scale.
Phase 3 — Working groups
Sector-specific groups to address concrete challenges: regulatory interpretation, contract norms, labor practices.
Phase 4 — Academic partnership
Collaboration with universities for more systematic research, grounding the dialogue in evidence.
六
现实限制 Reality Check
This approach has real limitations. We do not minimize them.
  • Strategic competition between the U.S. and China will not disappear. Government policies — export controls, investment restrictions — may constrain collaboration regardless of good intentions.
  • The media environment on both sides may continue to deepen mistrust, regardless of what any platform does.
  • Businesses may be reluctant to participate if benefits are unclear — or if participation carries reputational or political risk.
  • Similar dialogue platforms already exist. This proposal does not replace them, and cannot by itself change the international environment.
  • In China specifically, the space available for companies to engage openly with foreign counterparts may be constrained in ways that are difficult to anticipate.
But perhaps that is not the most important question. The absence of a stable platform is itself a problem worth addressing. Even partial, imperfect dialogue is better than its absence.
同舟共济

In the same boat, we row together.

The question is not whether the U.S. and China will compete. They will.

The real question is whether competition must become conflict —
or whether, through new forms of dialogue, we can find space for cooperation within rivalry.

如果两国真的在同一条船上，那么失败不会是各自的。而是共同的。
