同舟共济

Rethinking U.S.–China Business Dialogue

重新思考中美工商界对话

Scroll
一
陷阱 The Trap
历史告诉我们，当一个正在崛起的国家遇到一个已经强大的国家时，结果往往是冲突。
History tells us: when a rising power meets an established one, the result is often conflict.
古代的雅典和斯巴达。 Ancient Athens and Sparta.
一个在上升，一个在防守。 One ascending. One defending.
·
今天，很多人开始思考：美国和中国会不会进入类似的局面？ Today, many begin to ask: Could the United States and China enter a similar situation?
修昔底德陷阱 The Thucydides Trap When a rising power challenges an established one, structural tensions can produce catastrophic conflict. Harvard economist Graham Allison coined this term after studying 16 cases of power transitions in history.
历史让我们有理由担心。 History gives us reason for concern.
但是，历史并不一定决定未来。 But history does not have to determine the future.
中美关系的未来，还没有被写下。 The future of U.S.–China relations has not yet been written.
二
矛盾 The Contradiction
今天，中美关系在很大程度上由竞争来定义。 Today, the U.S.–China relationship is largely defined by competition.
贸易保护。科技竞争。战略上的不信任。 Trade protection. Technology competition. Strategic distrust.
在两国社会中，越来越多的声音把对方看成一种威胁，而不仅仅是竞争对手。 In both societies, more voices see the other not just as a rival, but as a threat.
这种情况让不信任感不断加深。 This deepens mistrust — not just between governments, but among ordinary people.
·
但是，在表面之下，还有另一种现实。 But beneath the surface — another reality persists.
虽然关系日益紧张，中美之间依然存在很深的联系。供应链仍然跨越两国。资金仍然在两国之间流动。创新的发展也依赖人才、知识和基础设施的共享。 Despite mounting tensions, deep connections remain between the two nations. Supply chains still cross borders. Capital still flows. Innovation still depends on shared talent, knowledge, and infrastructure.
你中有我，我中有你。 You are in me, and I am in you.
吴越同舟。 Two rivals, in the same boat.
但如果真是这样，如果两国联系如此紧密，为什么我们却感觉它们在渐渐远离？ But if both are so deeply linked — why does it feel like they are drifting apart?
这种相互依存关系是不是正在减弱？"脱钩"是不是已经在发生？如果是这样，这意味着什么？ Is interdependence weakening? Is decoupling already happening? And what does this mean?
三
体系的压力 A System Under Strain
传统上，我们认为政府会处理国家之间的关系。 Traditionally, we think governments manage relations between nations.
但政府更多关注的是安全、战略和政治压力。 But governments focus on security, strategy, and political pressure.
企业则不同。 Businesses are different.
它们关注的是风险、机会，以及长期发展。 They think about risk, opportunity, and long-term growth.
而现在，这两个世界正在慢慢分离。 And now, these two worlds are slowly separating.
Scholars like Meg Rithmire point out that political tensions are changing how corporations operate globally. Businesses face:
• Growing uncertainty • More complex policy environments • Rising geopolitical risks
Yet companies still want to develop, collaborate, and innovate.
Economic logic Political pressure
Where businesses now operate — caught between economic opportunity and government constraint
问题在于：目前没有一个稳定、可信的平台，让不同国家的企业、投资者和研究者可以公开交流这些问题。 The problem: no stable, trusted platform exists where businesses, investors, and researchers can openly discuss these tensions.
没有一个共同的空间，让大家可以更好地理解风险、机会和彼此的期待。 No shared space to understand risk, opportunity, and mutual expectations.
我们原本依赖的体系，正在承受越来越大的压力。 The systems we once relied on are under growing strain.
四
质疑 Pressure Test
即使我们尝试建立新的平台，也必须面对一些重要的质疑。 Any honest proposal must face hard questions.
我们是不是高估了企业在国家竞争中的作用？在历史上，经济上的相互依存，真的能防止冲突吗？还是有时候也会失败？ Have we overestimated business influence in state competition? In history, has economic interdependence truly prevented conflict — or has it sometimes failed?

利润还在。信心已经动摇。 Profits remain. Confidence has faltered. And confidence, historically, predicts the future before profits do.
细看，情况没有好多少。一项2025年的研究发现，双边贸易的下降，并不能真正反映两国经济联系的全貌。 A 2025 study found that declining bilateral trade does not capture the full picture:
很多商品如今通过墨西哥、越南、韩国等第三国中转。 Much trade now routes through Mexico, Vietnam, South Korea, and other intermediaries.
换句话说，两国都在努力减少直接依赖——而且在很大程度上，它们成功了。 In other words: both nations are reducing direct dependence — and they are largely succeeding.
脱钩没有消失。它只是被掩盖了。 Decoupling has not disappeared. It has only been obscured.
·
在科技领域——半导体、人工智能、先进制造——脱钩已经在发生，而且很可能无法逆转。 In technology — semiconductors, AI, advanced manufacturing — decoupling is already underway, likely irreversible.
这是因为两国政府都把技术能力和国家安全联系在了一起。 Both governments now link technological capability directly to national security.
在这些领域，竞争确实更接近零和。 In these sectors, competition truly approaches zero-sum.
对于企业来说，问题更加现实。参与这个平台，能带来什么实际价值？会不会带来名誉或政治风险？ For businesses, the questions are more practical: What concrete value would participation bring? Could it create reputational or political risk?
这些都不是理论问题。而是真实存在的限制。 These are not theoretical problems. They are real constraints.
五
提案 The Proposal
如果政府有其限制，市场本身也不够，那么，我们缺少的是什么？ If governments have their limits, and markets alone are insufficient — what is missing?
如果商界可以在稳定中美关系中发挥更大的作用，会怎么样？不是取代政府。而是作为一种补充。 What if business could play a larger role in stabilizing the relationship — not replacing government, but complementing it?
·
我们的想法是：建立一个面向企业、投资者和学术机构的对话平台。 Our proposal: a dialogue platform for businesses, investors, and academic institutions.
重点不是意识形态，而是实际的合作与协调。 Not ideological. Focused on practical cooperation and coordination.
这个平台有四个目标。 Four Core Objectives
一
Find Common Ground
Identify areas still open to collaboration — green energy, medical technology, supply chain resilience — while clearly marking "red lines" so companies can assess risk accurately.
二
Bridge Information Gaps
Help businesses understand each other's institutions, policies, and commercial cultures. Many cross-border failures stem not from malice but from misunderstanding.
三
Transform Public Narratives
Through short video and storytelling, show the realities of enterprises in both nations. Reduce stereotypes. Build more complete, nuanced understanding on both sides.
四
Build Lasting Dialogue
Not a one-time conference, but an ongoing process of exchange — so relationships can form gradually and trust can develop over time.
在实践中，可以分阶段进行。 In practice, this unfolds in phases:
Phase 1 — Short-Form Media
Videos introducing real business situations from both sides. Accessible, human, concrete — showing actual challenges and opportunities.
Phase 2 — Small-Group Dialogue
Intimate conversations among entrepreneurs, investors, and researchers from both nations. Deep exchange over large-scale events.
Phase 3 — Working Groups
Sector-specific teams addressing concrete challenges: regulatory interpretation, contract norms, labor practices, supply chain coordination.
Phase 4 — Academic Partnership
Collaboration with universities for systematic research, grounding dialogue in evidence and creating long-term institutional memory.
六
现实考量 Reality Check
当然，这种方法也有很多局限。 Of course, this approach has real limitations. We do not minimize them.
  • 中美之间的战略竞争不会消失。政府政策，比如出口管制或投资限制，可能会影响合作。 Strategic competition between the nations will not disappear. Government policy — export controls, investment restrictions — may constrain collaboration regardless of intentions.
  • 媒体环境和公众看法，也可能继续加深不信任。 The media environment and public opinion on both sides may continue deepening mistrust.
  • 企业本身也可能不愿意参与。如果没有明确的好处，这种参与可能被认为没有必要，甚至有风险。 Businesses themselves may be reluctant to participate. Without clear benefits, participation might seem unnecessary — or even risky.
  • 在中国的背景下，这些问题可能更加复杂。企业到底有多大的独立性？它们有多少空间可以与外国企业进行开放的交流？ In China's context, questions are more complex. How much independence do enterprises have? What space exists for open dialogue with foreign counterparts?
  • 在某些行业中，政治逻辑是不是已经超过了经济逻辑？ In certain sectors, has political logic already superseded economic logic?
  • 类似的对话平台已经存在。这个提案并不能取代它们。也不一定能从根本上改变整个国际环境。 Similar platforms already exist. This proposal does not replace them, and likely cannot fundamentally transform the international environment.
·
但也许，这并不是最重要的。 But perhaps that is not the most important question.
一个稳定、可信的平台的缺失本身，就是一个值得解决的问题。 The absence of a stable, trusted platform is itself a problem worthy of solving.
即使是不完美的对话，也比没有对话好。 Even imperfect dialogue is better than its absence.
七
结尾 Resolution
问题不在于中美会不会竞争。它们一定会竞争。 The question is not whether the U.S. and China will compete. They will.
真正的问题是：竞争是不是一定会变成冲突？ The real question: Must competition become conflict? Or can we, through new forms of dialogue, find space for cooperation within rivalry?
如果两国真的在同一条船上，那么失败不会是各自的。而是共同的。 If both nations truly share the same boat, then failure will not be theirs alone — it will be mutual.
·
真正的挑战，不只是竞争。而是学会在同一条船上，一起前进。 The true challenge is not competition itself — but learning to move forward together in the same boat.
